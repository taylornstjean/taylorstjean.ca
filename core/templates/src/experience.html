Taylor St Jean

I have considerable experience working in Python, LaTeX, CSS3, HTML5, and JavaScript, and I have familiarity with Mathematica, C++, and Bash. Using Python, I am actively developing track reconstruction software for the MATHUSLA experiment at CERN. I presented my progress on the algorithm at the University of Washington Mary Gates Undergraduate Research Symposium. I am also currently working on NEOSMAP, a web service designed to streamline near-Earth object follow-up observations. The project includes real-time monitoring of the Minor Planet Center near-Earth object confirmation database, creation of data visualization and consolidation tools, and frontend/backend development via Flask.
